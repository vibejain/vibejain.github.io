ML Vendor-Price Prediction: US Contract Manufacturer

A machine-learning model that predicts raw-material prices so a US contract manufacturer can quote contracts with confidence.

  • XGBoost
  • Forecasting
  • Feature Engineering
  • scikit-learn
  • Pricing

The Problem

A US contract manufacturer needed to project the price of raw materials when bidding and quoting on contracts. Pricing was largely manual and estimate-based, which made quotes risky: under-quote and you lose margin, over-quote and you lose the deal.

What I Built

Machine-learning models that forecast vendor and material prices — turning a gut-feel estimate into a data-driven projection. I engineered features from the manufacturer's historical pricing and materials data (material, shape, order volume, product type) and trained gradient-boosted models, validated with cross-validation, to surface a defensible price projection for each material.

How It Works

1 · DATACollect historical vendor & material pricing data.
2 · FEATURESEngineer the real cost drivers: material, shape, volume, product type.
3 · TRAINTrain & cross-validate gradient-boosted models.
4 · SERVEServe a defensible price projection via API.

Why It Matters

  • Helps the manufacturer project material pricing before committing to a contract.
  • Supports more accurate quoting and bidding, protecting margin.
  • Replaces manual guesswork with a repeatable, data-driven forecast.

Tech Stack

  • XGBoost / gradient boosting
  • scikit-learn
  • pandas
  • Feature engineering
  • FastAPI serving
